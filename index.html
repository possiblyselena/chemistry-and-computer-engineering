What are Printed Circuit Boards?

PCBs are flat boards made up of layers of insulation and conductive materials to make circuits and electronics compact and efficient. These insulating materials are usually made of fiber glass or expoxy and the conductive layers are copper. The copper layers are etched to make routes and make connections in the circuit. However, PCBs can corrode overtime usually by the oxidation of the copper. Added moisture leads the copper to rust, where I learned that last semester. The lesson explored how the copper goes through a redox reaction with the oxygen and creates rust from migrating through the water droplet. By understanding this concept, I can apply this to electronics when preventing corrosion and being mindful of the materials my PCB is made out of. The ways it creates moisture is from high humidity, pollutants like smoke, tempurature flucuations, and chemical exposure.
